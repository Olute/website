    Role of technology in business

    Information Technology (IT) plays a vital role in business and is responsible for the revolution that has taken place in the business world such as the dotcom era. In the US for example there are thousands who run their entire businesses online and the small businesses who have no online presence plan to achieve this. According to the research firm Clutch.co 94% of small business website would be mobile friendly. The internet and IT has enabled businesses to reach more potential customers, develop an interactive relation with clients, reduce costs, maximise profit, improve customer relations & efficiency, empower staff to do more, reduce overheads, reassign costs and talents to core business areas. The impact of IT has been well documented in areas such as; marketing using digital transformation, data-driven, mobile-ready approach. In operations the use of productivity tools to manage time, cost, resources and the whole project as a whole; In manufacturing the use of artificial intelligence to create innovative and state of the art products is becoming more and more popular. The finance teams are working more closely with IT and marketing to provide useful data that integrates with the Customer Relationship Management (CRM) system and generates much needed that drives marketing as well as production.

    The place of planning in IT projects

    Planning is an essential step in the project lifecycle and needs to be carried out at the start of the project. This provides a roadmap and a framework for the project to be carried out. The planning stage sets out the objective, scope and activities within the phases as well as the budget and resources required to achieve the tasks. There are a lot of tools available for planning purposes both online and offline. I have had the opportunity to use the following, Con fluence, Jira, projectmanager.com, Basecamp, slack and Trello. For this project Ie have used Trello both as a planning tool and as a means of communication between teams. It is an effective tool as it allows tasks to be displayed in Kanban style and items can be grouped based on assigned labels and interdependencies identified.     Selecting software and sytems for an IT project

    Before considering IT systems an organisation should establish its vision and what they would like to achieve from the digital transformation. This would determine the choice of systems and tools to deploy. In particular the areas to look at will include the following; System requirements The company goals How it integrates with existing system Providers and their ability to integrate into existing systems such as CRM Training required Their ability to keep up with future technology References from existing clients within the same industry The initial outlay viz a viz the Return On Investment (ROI) of implementing the new system

    Legal Constarints to be considered for an IT project

    With the use of IT in all major sectors including financial services, insurance, technology and especially social media. As a result of the amount of data and information been transmitted electronically there are laws and regulations to guide how data is used, processed and stored. The General Data Protection Regulation which came in to effect on 25th May 2018 has the following headlines to protect EU citizens; Requiring the consent of subjects for data processing Anonymizing collected data to protect privacy Providing data breach notifications Safely handling the transfer of data across borders Requiring certain companies to appoint a data protection officer to oversee GDPR compliance Simply put, the GDPR mandates a baseline set of standards for companies that handle EU citizens’ data to better safeguard the processing and movement of citizens’ personal data. In the UK the Information Commissioner office (ICO) is responsible to uphold information rights. Comp0anies have to register with the ICO to handle data and have to report breaches as when they occur. Non-compliance could result in fines and penalties. As a minimum organisation are required to seek consent for requesting, handling and storing personal information of data subjects
